Today’s reality is that many patients who live in remote areas are unable to receive in-person medical attention because they live so far from a health care provider. The current pandemic has made it even more difficult for patients to receive care. Our video conferencing service provides patients and physicians with the ability to meet for consultations from anywhere. It goes beyond just simple video conferencing; our solution provides real-time diagnosis abilities, the ability to track metrics such as length of stay, response times, and average length of stay, and an e-form to securely send patient information.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Have more questions?
Is telehealth safe?
Yes, telehealth is a safe and effective way to expand health care. Everything is held to the same standards as in-person visits, but in a safer and more convient way for both patients and practices by reducing the risk of exposure to infections. Your files and data are also kept in private and nothing will be shared or accessed by someone outside your organization without your permission. Contact us if you have questions about how we're accomodating your health and security concerns.
Where can patients get access to telehealth service?
Paitents can ask their employers, doctors, hospitals, or healthcare providers about telehealth services they may offer. Through ConnectHealth, we offer one centralized location for patients to access to telehealth information and resources.
Can ConnectHealth guarantee that their services will be effective for our practice?
Of course; we make sure to fix any issues and problems you might run into ourselves. All of our services are carefully designed to accomodate your own online platform and expand as your practice sees fit. Our staff is available at any time to assist with using our services and we are always working on ways to improve, so let us know what we can do to help make your experience using ConnectHealth better.
Why should I use telehealth services?
It is not always easy or convenient for patients to get help for their health care needs. The purpose of telecommunication is to provide safer, improved care for patients from the comforts of their home, especially during these times.
What is the difference between telemedicine and telehealth?
These two words are often used interchangeably; both terms refer to the use of technology to deliver healthcare in a way that is convenient for your paitents.
