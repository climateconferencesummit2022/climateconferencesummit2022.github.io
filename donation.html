Use Your Power

Legislative action is very important in the fight against climate change, so you can write to or call your representative to urge them to take action. Here are some pieces of legislation currently in Congress that you can write to your representatives about. Note: This section only provides information for US residents. But, if you live somewhere else, feel free to contact your own local and national representatives.

H.R.2310 - Climate Change Education Act

This bill, if passed, would require the administrator of the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) to establish a Climate Change Education Program. It was introduced in the House on 4/01/2021.

Bill Text: https://www.congress.gov/bill/117th-congress/house-bill/2310/text

H.R.2826 - To establish a Global Climate Change Resilience Strategy, to authorize the admission of climate-displaced persons, and for other purposes.

This bill was introduced in the House on 04/22/2021. This bill wants to create a Global Climate Change Resilience Strategy that will create a pathway to immigration into the US for those displaced by climate change.

Bill Text: https://www.congress.gov/bill/117th-congress/house-bill/2826/text

S.1956 - Manufacturing Clean Energy and Energy Efficiency Act of 2021

This bill is meant to require the Department of Energy to create a program that would give grants to small and medium sized companies that become more energy efficient or decrease their greenhouse gasses. It was introduced in the Senate on 05/28/2021.

Bill Text: https://www.congress.gov/bill/117th-congress/senate-bill/1956/text?r=59&s=1
