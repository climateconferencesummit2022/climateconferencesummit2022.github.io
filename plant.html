About the Event

One way for individuals to help the environment is by growing plants. More plants means more carbon dioxide taken from the atmosphere and turned into oxygen through photosynthesis. And, certain plants have even more specific benefits to the environment. The best part is, planting is something that can be done by anyone! To encourage this, we are hosting a Plant Together Event where people all over the world plant a flower or shrub on the same day, as an act of solidarity for our environment.

What Can You Plant?

While all plants are good for the environment, certain plants are better for the soil and purifying the air. Here is a list that we compiled of that are the best for the environment and easy to find.

More
Lavender, Allium, Cotoneaster, Foxglove

These plants are bee-friendly as they provide bees with the nectar and pollen they need. Bees are incredibly important for the environment so by planting lavender, you help purify the air while also helping bees.

Fruits and Vegetables

A fruit and vegetable garden is always a good idea. They help decrease pollution but they also help you become more self-sustaining. By using produce from your own garden, you don’t need to buy produce grown with pesticides that have polluted streams. You help the environment in multiple ways with a fruit and vegetable garden.

Aloe Vera, English Ivy, Spider Plants
This air-purifying plant is easy to grow and can thrive in many conditions. It can grow indoors and outdoors, so if you don’t have a garden, you can still grow this plant.
Any Tree Native to Your Region
Trees are wonderful for the environment. They live for decades and because they’re big, they filter the most amount of carbon dioxide from the environment.

Tips

1. Make sure that the plant you choose will thrive in the climate you’re in. Certain plants do better in certain temperatures and weather conditions and it’s important to keep that in mind when choosing your plant.

2. Ensure that your plant isn’t an invasive species to the area. We don’t want to hurt the environment through this project.

3. You can either plant seeds or seedlings. They need to be planted in different ways, so make sure to look at the instructions that came with the plant or research your specific plant.

4. Choose a spot for your plant that has optimal sunlight during the day and is close to a water source to make sure that your plant really thrives. For an additional benefit, choose a location that you see all the time so you don’t forget about your plant.

5. When it comes to watering your plant, make sure you don’t do it too much or too little. The standard rule is to water your plants 1 inch per week, maybe more during summer. But, like with everything, this is subjective to each plant.
